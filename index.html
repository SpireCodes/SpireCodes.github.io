About Me

I'm Spire, I work as an engineer at a consultancy and I build keyboards on stream on my Twitch channel here: Twitchspire_blues

Image here?

My commissions are mostly focussed towards people with little knowledge about keyboards, so I'm used to guiding them towards their ideal build. I keep budget under control by showing people their options when it comes to price vs quality.

That said, I can also work with more complicated builds and I'm happy to have conversations with knowledgeable people who can provide a challenge!

My first keyboard!
It was made out of cheap off-brand parts from Aliexpress with MX Cherry Red switches soldered onto it.

My second keyboard
This is the Quefrency rev2 with MX Cherry Brown switches soldered onto it. The keycaps are a combination of 2 sets of blank Cherry profile keycaps.

Commission
The PCB for this is the KBD67 rev2 with Sakurios switches placed in hotswap (Millmax) sockets, inside the Tofu65 frosted acrylic case. The keycaps are Cherry profile KP Republic FR with some Tai-Hao rubber accents.

Extra parts build
Like the name suggests, this keyboard was made from leftover parts! The PCB for this is a slightly modded KBD67 rev2 with Kailh Speed Copper switches placed in hotswap (Millmax) sockets, inside a standard KBDFans 65% aluminium frosted acrylic case. The keycaps are blank KAT profile switches.

Commission
This is the Quefrency rev2 with Kailh Pro Burgundy switches soldered onto it. The keycaps are the leftover keys from my second keyboard.

Personal macropad
This is the Kyria with Kailh Choc White switches soldered onto it. The underglow LEDs were hand-soldered by me.

First attempt at designing a macropad
This was meant to be a fully hotswap (Kailh sockets) macropad with per-key and underglow LEDs and an inbuilt MCU. Unfortunately, the electronics surrounding the MCU were designed incorrectly so this PCB did not work.

Second attempt at designing a macropad
This PCB uses Promicro microcontroller breakout header pins instead of having an onboard MCU. For this iteration I also added a front and back plate for aesthetics. Unfortunately, I used the wrong schematic symbol for the per-key LEDs, so they don't work. Everything else does, though!
